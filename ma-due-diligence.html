// Assessment scope

Full infrastructure teardown.

01

Domain Portfolio Review

Complete inventory of every domain asset associated with the target: outbound domains, transactional domains, legacy domains, subdomains, and parked assets that may carry sending history or reputation debt. Domain sprawl is consistently underreported in M&A documentation. We map ownership, registration status, expiration risk, and purpose separation — then assess whether the portfolio is structured to survive integration or likely to create consolidation conflicts post-close.

02

Identity and Authentication

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC posture assessed for current state and migration readiness. Root vs subdomain strategy and delegation reviewed. Risk flags: shared identity, misalignment, weak enforcement policy, governance gaps. A rebrand or domain consolidation that breaks authentication can disable sending within days of close.

03

Mailbox Infrastructure and Reputation

Inbox provider distribution, mailbox configuration, and operational consistency reviewed. Reputation exposure points identified: domain reuse, overlap, weak isolation, shared sending identity. Blacklist status and complaint signal history assessed. Acquired reputation debt does not reset on ownership transfer.

04

Sending Architecture and Volume Design

Step pacing, ramp logic, and concurrency reviewed for sustainability. Cadence structure assessed for complaint risk, bounce risk, and reply dilution. List hygiene standards and suppression logic evaluated. Systems sending at unsustainable volume to hit numbers create inherited liability that surfaces immediately after close.

05

Tooling, Stack, and CRM Handoff Integrity

Outbound platform and ESP configuration reviewed at the architectural level. CRM handoff points, lead routing logic, and suppression sync assessed for integration risk. What breaks if the two systems are merged, migrated, or consolidated — and in what sequence it breaks.

06

Enforcement and Continuity Risk

What is currently manual, undocumented, or unenforced. Where operating rules exist only in the heads of people who may not survive the transition. What governance exists in writing vs what disappears with key personnel. This is consistently the highest post-close risk category.

07

Migration and Integration Risk Mapping

Specific failure analysis for likely post-close scenarios: domain migration, rebrand, CRM consolidation, platform migration, or team restructuring. Staged against your integration timeline with clear dependencies. What must be stabilized before anything is merged.

! Note

Email infrastructure risk is silent until it isn't. Blacklisted domains, broken authentication, and degraded sending reputation don't appear in financial statements.
