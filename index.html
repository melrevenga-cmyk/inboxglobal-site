// Outbound Email Reliability Checklist

60-second
self-assessment.

Answer "Not sure" to 4 or more of these and your system is operating on luck — not architecture.

If you answer "Not sure" to 4+, the system is operating on luck.
A / Separation
  • Outbound domains are separate from core brand, product, and transactional email.
  • Inbox identities are not reused across teams, motions, or offers.
  • Streams are segmented by purpose — not mixed.
B / Pacing
  • Volume increases are controlled and gradual — not driven by hiring pushes.
  • Sequences have hard caps to prevent silent flooding.
  • You have a clear send calendar with no unexpected spike days.
C / Enforcement
  • Rules are written — who can send, from where, under what limits.
  • Rules are enforced with a clear owner. Exceptions are rare and documented.
  • Changes follow a change log with what changed, when, why, and who approved.
D / Monitoring + Reporting
  • You track leading indicators — not just replies.
  • You have thresholds that trigger action.
  • Weekly reporting shows what changed and what action was taken.
E / Handoff + Infrastructure
  • Ownership is explicit across setup, daily ops, and exceptions.
  • Continuity is real: docs, rules, access, and onboarding exist — not tribal knowledge.
  • You have a sender inventory: every platform that can send email is documented.
