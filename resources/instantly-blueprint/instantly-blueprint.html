The Generic Domain Architecture Framework

Your sending domains must be entirely generic — no mention of your brand name, product, or service. Generic domains protect your primary brand's reputation and avoid signalling to spam filters that a recognisable brand is cold emailing en masse.

🚫
Hard rule: Never send cold email from your primary brand domain. One spam complaint from a cold prospect can blacklist your entire business domain, killing deliverability across all channels — not just outreach.
⚠
Avoid branded variations too. Domains like getAcme.com or tryAcme.io still expose your brand to reputation risk. Use entirely unrelated, generic names.

📐 The Golden Ratio Rule
1 Domain : 2–3 Mailboxes
Never exceed 3 mailboxes per domain. Beyond this, domain reputation degrades under shared sending load. Spread across more domains instead.
⏳ Domain Age Progression

Domain age is one of the strongest trust signals. Register domains in batches and let them age — this is not a step you can rush.
🔄 Domain Rotation & Retirement
Register batches of 5–10 generic domains at once. Let them age 30 days before warmup. Rotate retiring domains out at the first sign of reputation drops — never try to "recover" a burned domain. Domain cost is cheap; reputation damage is expensive.
ℹ
All three records — SPF, DKIM, and DMARC — must be correctly configured before starting warmup. Instantly will flag issues in your mailbox health panel.

✓ SPF Best Practices
SPF has a hard limit of 10 DNS lookups total — every include:, a:, and mx mechanism counts. Exceeding 10 causes a PermError and SPF fails silently. Only one SPF TXT record per domain is permitted — multiple v=spf1 records break authentication. If you use several sending services, use an SPF flattening tool (dmarcian, EasyDMARC) to stay under the limit. Use ~all (softfail) on sending domains — -all (hardfail) is too aggressive for new domains and can cause legitimate mail to be rejected.
✓ DKIM Key Rotation
Rotate DKIM keys every 6–12 months as hygiene. Old exposed keys are a security risk. Add this to your domain management calendar as a recurring task.
→ MX Records Required
Set MX records correctly even on sending-only domains. Receiving capability signals legitimacy to ESPs. A domain that can't receive email looks disposable to spam filters.

📄 Send as Plain Text — Always

HTML email contains embedded styling, image tags, and tracking pixels that spam filters actively scan. Plain text emails bypass these checks entirely and feel like a real human sent them from their inbox — because that's exactly what they look like.

✓
In Instantly: Campaign Settings → Email Format → set to Plain Text. No bold, no bullet points, no HTML signature images. Just text.
🖊️ Plain Text Signature Setup

Your signature must also be plain text. No company logos, no banner images, no LinkedIn icons. A clean 2-line signature — name and role/company only. No phone number: it adds no value in cold email and can inadvertently signal telemarketing patterns to filters.

Replies are your real signal. Open rates in cold email are a vanity metric that costs you deliverability. If you use no links — which is the recommended approach — click tracking is moot entirely.

ESP Enrichment — Match Your Sender to Your Prospect's Inbox

One of the highest-leverage infrastructure moves available to operators: identifying which ESP your prospect uses before you send, and routing their email to the matched sending provider. Most teams skip this entirely.

🔎 How to Get ESP Data
Lead export  Many list tools (Apollo, Sales Navigator exports) include MX data or company email infrastructure tags. Check your export columns — ESP data is often already there.
Clay enrichment  Use Clay's MX lookup enrichment to resolve the email domain to its mail provider (Google, Microsoft, custom). Map this to a column: esp_type. This runs at list-build time — no manual work per contact.
Manual MX check  For Tier 1 accounts, run mxtoolbox.com on the prospect's domain. The MX record reveals their mail provider directly.
📁 Uploading ESP-Matched Lists to Instantly

Once your lead file has an esp_type column, segment before uploading:

→ Filter for esp_type = Google → upload to campaign using GWS mailboxes only
→ Filter for esp_type = Microsoft → upload to M365 mailbox pool
→ Unknown / custom → default to Google Workspace
✓
This can be done at campaign creation in Instantly by assigning only the matching mailbox pool to each segmented campaign upload.
📈 Why ESP Matching Matters

Email service providers have preferential routing relationships within their own ecosystems. When a Google Workspace sender emails a Gmail recipient, the message travels within Google's infrastructure — trust signals, authentication checks, and reputation signals are all evaluated within the same network.

When you send from Google to Outlook or Outlook to Gmail, the message crosses provider boundaries. Every boundary crossing adds a filtering evaluation layer.

Operator Observed
ESP-matched campaigns consistently show 10–20% improvement in inbox placement rate vs. unmatched sends to the same lead pool — with no change to copy, sequence, or list quality.

The cost of ESP enrichment via Clay or lead export is minimal. The infrastructure investment — running both GWS and M365 mailbox pools — is the standard blended approach already. Segmenting by ESP at upload is the execution step most operators skip.

Disable open tracking by default. Open tracking relies on a 1x1 pixel image — a well-known spam signal. Many modern clients block or auto-fire it. The data is unreliable. The cost to deliverability is real.

🔗 Why a Custom Tracking Domain Is Non-Negotiable

When you use Instantly's default tracking domain, your link clicks are wrapped through a shared URL that thousands of other senders are also using. If any of those senders get flagged, their spam reputation attaches to the same tracking domain your campaigns use.

The risk: A single blacklisted sender on a shared tracking domain can cause your links to be flagged — even if your own sending behavior is clean. You're sharing reputation with strangers.

→ Custom domains look legitimate; generic shared domains look like bulk mail infrastructure
→ Use one tracking domain per tier — isolate Tier 1 reputation from Tier 3 entirely

Warmup Mechanics

Warmup isn't a checkbox. It's an ongoing, calibrated process. Here's how Instantly's warmup network actually works and how to configure it correctly.

What Actually Happens in Instantly's Warmup Network

When you enable warmup, Instantly automatically sends low-volume emails between accounts in its warmup pool — a network of 4.2M+ real mailboxes. These emails are automatically opened, moved out of spam if they land there, and replied to. This activity signals to Gmail/Outlook that your domain sends legitimate, engaged email.

ℹ
Warmup emails are sent from a dedicated internal address, not your campaign address. Your campaign identity stays entirely separate from pool activity.
The key insight: Warmup doesn't improve reputation in isolation — it demonstrates positive engagement patterns. What matters is signal quality: low bounces, high opens, and replies. Instantly's pool generates all three automatically.
🏊 Why Pool Quality Matters

Not all warmup pools are equal. Instantly's pool consists of real business accounts — not disposable addresses. Gmail and Outlook can detect warmup from throwaway accounts. Positive engagement from real inboxes carries significantly more weight with ESPs.

🔄 Warmup vs. Campaign Ratio
Day 1–14:   100% warmup / 0% campaign
Day 15–30:  Warmup ongoing + 5 campaign/day (ramp up)
Day 30–60:  Warmup ongoing + scale to 15–20/day
Day 60–90:  Warmup maintained + 30–40 campaign/day
Day 90+:    Full capacity — warmup at 20/day, never off
Never fully disable warmup — run it continuously as a permanent health signal, even at scale.

90-Day Warmup Ramp Schedule

InboxGlobal standard: warmup begins day one. Campaign sends are introduced at Day 15 (Week 3) at 5 emails/day — not Day 30. This controlled early entry builds sending patterns while infrastructure is still maturing. Erratic volume kills deliverability — consistent daily patterns are what ESPs learn to trust.

📅 Phase 1: Days 1–14
Warmup only. Zero campaign sends. Target warmup score: 70+ before proceeding. Monitor spam rescue rate daily — if warmup emails consistently land in spam, fix DNS before moving to Phase 2.
📅 Phase 2: Day 15–30
Introduce campaigns at 5 emails/day. This is the controlled entry point. Use only your most verified, cleanest Tier 1 leads. Warmup continues running in parallel throughout.
📅 Phase 3: Days 30–60
Scale campaign sends to 15–20/day, increasing by 5/day each week if warmup score holds above 80. Introduce Tier 2 lists. Continue warmup at full rate.
📅 Phase 4: Days 60–90+
Scale to 30–40 campaign sends/day. Never exceed 40 per mailbox. Warmup maintained at 20–25/day indefinitely. At 90 days, domain is mature — monitor weekly, not daily.

Campaign Architecture

Campaign architecture is where execution separates from infrastructure. This module covers branching logic, engagement-based routing, and multi-inbox rotation that reacts to how prospects actually behave.

📊
2026 Benchmark finding: 58% of all replies in cold email campaigns are generated from Step 1. Your first email is your highest-leverage asset. Treat it accordingly.

New leads go to the front of the queue

When you add new leads to an active campaign in Instantly, set them to start from Step 1 immediately — don't let them queue behind stale contacts. The longer a lead sits uncontacted after you've identified them, the less relevant your context becomes.

When uploading new leads to an active campaign: set the start step to Step 1, not "continue from where left off." Fresh leads get the first email, not a middle follow-up out of context.
📐 Step 1 priorities

58% of replies come from Step 1. This is where your testing budget goes — not on follow-ups.

→ A/B test Step 1 openers weekly — change one variable at a time
→ Keep it under 80 words. Not a goal — a constraint
→ One observation, one soft question. No pitch.
→ No links. Links in Step 1 cost deliverability and signal "mass email"
→ Use Instantly's preview and send test before activating any new variant
✓ What actually works in Step 1

The highest-performing Step 1 emails share one thing: they sound like a person thought about this specific recipient. Not a system. Not a playbook.

Do  Open with something specific to them — a recent hire, a launch, a role change
Do  Write like you talk. Read it out loud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it
Do  End with a soft question, not a pitch or a meeting ask
Do  Stay under 80 words. Every word that doesn't earn its place comes out
Don't  Open with your company name or what you do
Don't  Include links — none. Even "helpful" ones hurt deliverability and shift the tone
Don't  Use phrases like "just checking in", "hope this finds you well", or "quick question"
🔬 Test before you send — use Instantly's preview and deliverability score

Before activating any Step 1 campaign, use Instantly's built-in send test and deliverability score tools:

Preview  Check how the email renders across providers — especially Gmail and Outlook
Send test  Send to your own inbox first. Read it as a recipient, not as the sender
Deliverability score  Run the score check before launch — catch auth issues, spam signals, or formatting problems before they cost you sends

📊 Follow-Up Math
The 2026 benchmark confirms follow-ups account for 42% of total replies — worth the effort. But the first follow-up captures the majority of those. Sequences of 4–7 steps spaced 3–7 days apart optimize total reply rate. 70% of senders stop after one email and leave this pipeline on the table.

Multi-Inbox Rotation Architecture

Pool multiple mailboxes into a single campaign to scale send volume while keeping each mailbox under its daily limit. This is the primary scaling mechanism in Instantly.

⚙️ Rotation Config in Instantly
Campaign Settings → Email Accounts → add all mailboxes. Instantly distributes sends automatically. Each mailbox maintains its own identity — prospects receive from the assigned sender, not a shared address.
🎯 Sender Persona Consistency
Each sending mailbox needs: a profile photo in Google Workspace, a plain-text email signature, and a completed LinkedIn profile associated with the sending domain. Persona believability is a trust signal.
🔴 When Rotation Goes Wrong
If one mailbox in your pool gets flagged, pause that account only — not the entire campaign. Audit leads that mailbox sent to for bad data. Replace it with a pre-warmed spare. Always maintain a 20% bench of spare warmed mailboxes for continuity.

🗂️

Step 3: CRM Push SLA: <5 min

Create the CRM record now — not after the meeting is booked. Log the original sequence, reply text, and intent signal. This gives you full pipeline visibility from the moment of first positive signal. Tag: opportunity-open. Waiting until after booking means you're blind to conversion drop-off between interested and booked.

▼
✍️

Step 4: Personalised Human Reply SLA: <1 hour

The first reply from a human must happen within 1 hour. Speed-to-reply is the single biggest driver of conversion from interested to booked. Use the prospect's exact words — mirror their language.

▼
📅

Step 5: Meeting Sell — Not a Demo Sell SLA: same reply

Frame the meeting as an audit, not a product demo. "15-minute deliverability review of your current setup" outperforms "I'd love to show you our platform." Give the prospect a concrete outcome they take from the call regardless of whether they buy.

▼
🎯

Step 6: Specific Time Offer + Calendar Link SLA: same reply

Offer two specific time slots: "Are you available Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 10am?" This reduces cognitive load compared to an open calendar link. Only send the Calendly URL as a fallback if they don't pick one of your two options. Update CRM: tag handover-complete.

Compliance Framework

As of 2026, Gmail, Yahoo, and Microsoft have all enforced bulk sender rules. Compliance is no longer optional — it's infrastructure.

🚨
2026 enforcement update: Microsoft joined Google and Yahoo in May 2025 with Outlook.com bulk sender requirements. Non-compliant senders are moved to Junk then rejected entirely. These rules are now enforced at the ESP level — there is no opt-out.

Gmail Sender Requirements (Enforced 2024–2026)

✅
SPF Authentication Required
Publish a valid SPF record for your sending domain. Gmail verifies this on every inbound email. Failure results in delivery to spam or rejection.
✅
DKIM Authentication Required
Your sending domain must have a DKIM signature. The From: domain must align with either SPF or DKIM for DMARC compliance.
✅
DMARC Policy Required
Publish a DMARC record at minimum p=quarantine. p=none is no longer acceptable at scale — it provides no protection and signals weak security posture. Full rejection at p=reject is the gold standard but requires SPF/DKIM alignment confidence.
⚠️
Spam Rate: Stay Well Below 0.3%
Gmail's Postmaster Tools mitigation threshold is 0.3%. The best operators target ≤0.1%. Exceeding 0.3% triggers deliverability restrictions — no warning, immediate enforcement.
ℹ️
RFC 8058 One-Click Unsubscribe (Bulk Senders)
Required for senders sending 5,000+ emails/day to personal Gmail accounts. Add List-Unsubscribe and List-Unsubscribe-Post headers. Instantly can inject these automatically — enable in campaign settings.
ℹ️
From: Domain Alignment
The domain in your From: address must align with the domain authenticated by SPF or DKIM. Misalignment is a common configuration error when using third-party sending tools.

What "Bulk Sender" Means in 2026

Google defines a bulk sender as any sender who sends 5,000+ messages to personal Gmail accounts (@gmail.com) within a 24-hour period, counted at the primary domain level across all sending subdomains.

CAN-SPAM (US) — B2B Cold Email Rules

✅
Identify yourself accurately
Your From:, To:, and Reply-To: fields must accurately represent who you are. No deceptive headers.
✅
Honest subject lines
Subject lines must not be deceptive. "Re:" or "Fwd:" prefixes on cold emails you haven't actually received are deceptive under CAN-SPAM — and also stopped working in 2026 as noted in benchmark data.
✅
Include physical address
CAN-SPAM requires a valid postal address in commercial emails. Use your business address or a registered mail forwarding address in your email signature.
✅
Honor opt-outs within 10 business days
Any unsubscribe request must be honored within 10 business days. Instantly's suppression list handles this automatically when opt-out links are enabled.

GDPR (EU/UK) — B2B Cold Email Considerations

⚠
This is not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for GDPR compliance guidance specific to your situation.
📋
Legitimate Interest — The B2B Basis
GDPR allows cold email to businesses under "legitimate interest" as a lawful basis — but only when the email is genuinely relevant to the recipient's professional role and you've conducted a Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA).
📋
Only Relevant Business Data
Use business email addresses (not personal Gmail/Hotmail) for EU outreach. The processing must be directly relevant to the individual's professional capacity.
📋
Opt-Out Must Be Easy and Immediate
Include a clear unsubscribe mechanism in every email. EU opt-out requests must be honored without delay. Store suppression lists securely and apply them across all campaigns.
